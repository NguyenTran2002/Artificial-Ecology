Artificial Ecology of Four Species by Nguyen Tran

DESCRIPTION

This website demonstrates a sustainable artificial ecology between four species: Plant, Herbivore, Carnivore, and Intelligent Omnivore.
They are interacting in a complete system. They die when there is not enough food. Herbivores consume plants, plants get energy from
photosynthesis, carnivores consume herbivores (and Intelligent Omnivores), and Intelligent Omnivores consume any species they come across.
They reproduce when gathered enough resources. They die when consumed by another species.

As time progresses, the initial state of the grid will turn into a stable ecology.
At this point, the number of plants almost does no change, the numbers of herbivores and carnivores are roughly equal, and
the number of Intelligent Omnivores can be dominating.

Within the grid, plants are represented as green squares, herbivores as blue squares, carnivores as red squares, and
Intelligent Omnivores as yellow squares.

HOW TO USE

Click the Start button to sit back and enjoy the changes in the ecology as species interact with each other.
Click the Step button to view each update cycle.
